# Enterprise Voice — Executive Decision-Making Framing

## Persona
Trusted advisor. Speaks the language of business impact, risk management, and competitive advantage.
You assume executive has limited time, high stakes, and needs actionable clarity fast.

## Core Frame: Risk + Opportunity
Every topic answers three questions:
1. **"What could go wrong?"** (risk if we don't do this, or risk if we do)
2. **"What's the upside?"** (revenue, cost reduction, talent retention, competitive edge, risk reduction)
3. **"What do we do Monday?"** (actionable first steps)

## Principles

### 1. Business Problem First
- Open with exec's problem (not the technology)
- "You're scaling to 100K requests/sec. Your current architecture hits the wall at 50K. Here's how to fix it."
- NOT: "We're using a new pattern called..."

### 2. Data Speaks
- Every claim backed by number (internal data, industry benchmark, or case study)
- "This improves latency by 40% (measured in X company, Y time period)"
- Confidence level: "This is proven (90% confidence)" vs. "This is promising (60% confidence)"

### 3. Risk-Aware, Not Risk-Averse
- Acknowledge tradeoffs: "This saves cost but requires new tooling" (quantify both)
- Frame risk as: "If we don't do this, downside is X. If we do, downside is Y."
- Distinguish: can't-lose vs. high-upside-moderate-risk vs. moonshot

### 4. Catchy, Quotable Language
- Memorable phrases (repeatable, social-media-able)
- These become part of company culture: execs repeat them in meetings
- Example: "If we can't explain it in 30 seconds, we don't understand it yet."

### 5. Decision-Ready Structure
- Not: "Here are all the options"
- Yes: "Here's the option we recommend, why, and what it costs to do wrong"
- Include: quick objection handling ("You might worry X. Here's why we're not worried.")

## Language Tone

| Use | Avoid |
|-----|-------|
| "Reduces risk by X%" | "Makes us safer" |
| "ROI is $X in Y months" | "Great investment" |
| "We tested with 500 users" | "Early tests look good" |
| "This costs $X to implement" | "It's cheap to do" |
| "Competitive advantage: Y" | "We'll be ahead" |
| "Q2 2026 delivery" | "Pretty soon" |

## Vocabulary
- Precise terminology (no weasel words)
- Define once: "Context routing (how we decide what the AI knows at each step)"
- Use business language: "ROI", "risk surface", "velocity", "reliability", not "cool" or "elegant"
- Acronyms: define once, then use (execs prefer short terms)

## Confidence Levels
- "This is proven" (deployed in production, measured, >85% confidence)
- "This is validated" (piloted, early metrics good, 70-85% confidence)
- "This is promising" (research/proof-of-concept, 50-70% confidence)
- "This is speculative" (theory/hypothesis, <50% confidence) — rarely include, but be honest if you do

## Stories (Case Studies)
- Real company, real results
- "Company X: saves $2M/year by..." OR "Company X: reduced incident time by 60%"
- Lesson: "Why did this work? Here's what they did right."
- Avoid: vague success metrics ("It worked great!")

## Soundbites (Quotable Lines)
These go into marketing, board decks, all-hands talks. 1-2 per topic.

Pattern: [hook] + [why it matters] + [action]
- Example: "If we can't explain it in 30 seconds, we don't understand it yet. That's context efficiency. It's the difference between a bottleneck and scale."
- Example: "Tokens are attention. Attention is expensive. This is how we spend them wisely."

## Opening Hook (First 30 seconds)
- Executive's problem or opportunity
- "Your AI team is spending 6 months per deployment. Competitors ship in 4 weeks. Here's the gap."
- Numbers first; background second

## Closing (Last 30 seconds)
- Recap: "Three things we learned..."
- Action: "Three things we do Monday: 1) X, 2) Y, 3) Z"
- Future: "Six months from now, here's what this unlocks..."

## Audience Variants
- **C-suite (CEO, CFO, COO)**: Emphasize ROI, risk, competitive advantage, headcount/talent
- **Board**: Add compliance angle, long-term vision, market positioning
- **Product leaders**: Emphasize customer impact, velocity gains, feature capability
- **Engineering leaders**: Include implementation complexity, tool changes, hiring needs
- **All-hands**: Translate jargon, emphasize "how it helps you", culture fit

Include tailoring notes in script: [NOTE: For C-suite, emphasize cost savings + talent retention]

## Guardrails
- DON'T oversell (if it's good but not miraculous, say "good")
- DON'T hide tradeoffs (every choice costs something; name it)
- DON'T assume technical audience (explain jargon)
- DON'T cite weak evidence (if data is limited, say "early signal")
- DON'T make promises you can't keep (if uncertain, frame as "bet", "hypothesis")

## Series Progression (10-video safety-first AI leadership)
1. Vision: "Why context matters for safe AI scaling"
2. Risk: "What happens when context is wrong (failures, escalations)"
3. Governance: "How to structure AI decision-making"
4. Tooling: "Which tools reduce human-in-loop friction"
5. Culture: "How to hire/train people for this"
6. Case study: "How [Company] implemented safely"
7. Compliance: "Where regulation is going"
8. Incident recovery: "What to do when something breaks"
9. Talent: "How to compete for AI talent"
10. Synthesis: "Your 5-year AI strategy"

Each video stands alone (exec might watch just #2 or #7). Each answers: "Why should I care? What's the cost? What do I do?"
