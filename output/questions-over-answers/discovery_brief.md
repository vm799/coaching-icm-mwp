## Topic Summary

AI has made answers cheap and instant. Google, ChatGPT, Perplexity — anyone can get an answer in seconds. The scarce resource has shifted: it's no longer the answer. It's the question.

The expert of the AI age isn't the one with the most knowledge. It's the one who knows what to ask, when, and how. This maps directly to how ICM works — context management is question engineering at scale.

**Why now:** AI adoption is mainstream. But most organisations are optimising for the wrong thing (more AI, better answers) rather than the right thing (better questions, clearer context). This is the gap V can close.

**Core thesis:**
> "In a world where answers are cheap, questions are precious. The expert advantage isn't knowledge anymore — it's question quality."

## Dual Stream Angles

### Teaching Stream Angle
**Audience:** BAs, managers, engineers new to ICM/MWP

**Learning frame:** ICM isn't a tool — it's a question-engineering system. Every stage (discovery, research, script, multiply) is asking a better question at a different layer. When you understand this, the whole system clicks.

**Hook:** "You've been taught that expertise means having the right answers. AI just made that obsolete. Here's what expertise looks like now."

**Learning outcomes:**
1. Learner can explain why question quality > answer quality in AI systems
2. Learner can identify where questions are implicit in their current workflow
3. Learner can articulate how ICM stages are question layers
4. Learner can apply "question-first thinking" to prompt design

**Analogies ready (from analogies_bank.md):**
- "Questions are gold" — the expert as questioner, not answerer
- "AI is 70 years old" — sets expectation: not magic, engineered
- "Google gives you an answer. An expert gives you the right question to ask next."

**Complexity progression:**
- Simple: What changed? (Answers became cheap → questions became valuable)
- Medium: What is a good question? (Specific, contextual, progressive)
- Advanced: How ICM structures question-asking across stages

---

### Enterprise Stream Angle
**Audience:** C-suite, product leaders, board — deciding AI strategy

**Business frame:** Question quality is the AI moat. Teams that master this deploy faster, cheaper, more accurately than teams that just add AI tools.

**Hook:** "Your competitors are all buying the same AI tools. The ones who win aren't asking better AI — they're asking better questions of it."

**Executive takeaway:** Invest in question infrastructure (ICM), not just AI infrastructure (models, APIs, compute).

**Business impact:**
- Faster deployments (clear questions → clear context → accurate outputs → fewer iterations)
- Lower costs (selective context → fewer tokens → lower API spend)
- Better outcomes (right question → right answer first time, not fifth time)
- Sustainable moat (question quality compounds; model access doesn't)

**Soundbites ready (from quotes_raw.md):**
- "In a world where answers are cheap, questions are precious."
- "Experts are the ones that can ask the right questions."
- "Your moat isn't AI adoption. It's question quality."

**Risk angle:** Teams that don't develop question infrastructure will be overrun by those who do — not because they have less AI, but because they're getting lower-quality outputs from the same AI.
