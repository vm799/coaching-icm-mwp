## Session Types

### Type A: Executive 1:1 Briefing (30-45 min)
For: CEO, CFO, CTO, board member, prospect
Goal: Help one exec understand this topic well enough to make a decision
Format: Conversation > presentation. 5 slides max. Lots of Q&A.

**Session plan:**
- 0-5 min: Context setting (why this session, what they'll get out of it)
- 5-10 min: Problem framing (what's at stake for THEM)
- 10-20 min: Key concepts + case study (2-3 slides)
- 20-30 min: Q&A + their specific situation (open discussion)
- 30-35 min: Action items (what do THEY do next?)

**Prep notes:**
- Research: their company, their role, known pain points (15 min research before session)
- Customize 3 slides with their context (industry, company size, relevant data)
- Prepare 3 opening questions to understand their POV
- Have: 1-page leave-behind they can share with their team

### Type B: Team Workshop (60-90 min)
For: Technical or functional team (5-20 people)
Goal: Team understands AND can apply this
Format: Presentation + exercises + Q&A. Hands-on.

**Session plan:**
- 0-10 min: Frame the session (why this, what you'll do, how it's structured)
- 10-30 min: Core concepts (teaching voice — analogies + examples)
- 30-50 min: Hands-on exercise or live demo (see `references/demo_template.md`)
- 50-70 min: Q&A + team discussion (what does this mean for OUR work?)
- 70-80 min: Takeaways + next steps (what does each person do next week?)

**Prep notes:**
- Exercise: prepare worked example using THEIR codebase or workflow if possible
- Handout: printed or digital version of key concepts (they can keep)
- Follow-up: commit to sending resources within 24h

### Type C: Recorded Webinar (30-60 min async)
For: Larger audience; they watch on their time
Goal: Same as YouTube but in webinar format (more structured, less edited)
Format: Presentation + Q&A (if live) or just presentation (if async)

**Session plan:** Same as stage talk structure — see `references/stage_talk_template.md`

**Diff from YouTube:**
- Slides more prominent (screen share, not just speaker)
- Less polished production (webinar = practical, YouTube = polished)
- Platform-specific: attendee list, follow-up emails, replay access

## Opening Script

**121 opening:**
> "Thanks for the time. I want to make this useful for you specifically. Before I start — what's the one thing you most want to understand or decide by the end of this call?"
[Listen. Adjust.]

**Workshop opening:**
> "We're going to cover X today. By the end, you'll be able to Y. This is hands-on — I need you engaged, not passive. We'll have [demo / exercise / Q&A] so come ready to think."

**Webinar opening:**
> "Welcome. Today's session covers X. I'll keep it to [30/60] min. Recording will be shared. Questions in chat — I'll answer at the end or during breaks."

## Q&A Script

**When you don't know:**
> "Good question. I don't have a confident answer on that — I'd rather say I don't know than guess. I'll research it and send you a follow-up."

**When question is off-topic:**
> "That's a different but interesting thread — do you want to set up a separate call? Or I can send resources."

**When question is long/complex:**
> "Let me make sure I understand what you're asking..." [paraphrase back] "Is that right?"

**When question reveals a misunderstanding:**
> "Actually, let me clarify — I may not have explained X clearly enough..."

**When nobody asks questions:**
> "I'll ask one back to the room — [specific question from topic]. What do you think?"

Reference `references/demo_template.md` for full demo structure.

**Live demo rule: always have a backup.**
- Backup: screenshots of demo steps (show if live fails)
- Backup backup: "Walk me through it verbally — imagine the screen shows X, and we type Y..."

**Demo structure in live session:**
1. "Let me show you what this looks like in practice." [transition cue]
2. [Run demo — narrate every action as you do it]
3. "Notice [key thing]" [point out what matters]
4. "Any questions on what you just saw?" [pause]
5. "Let me show the same thing from a different angle..." [if time allows]

## Async Version (Loom / Recorded Video, No Live Audience)

When audience can't attend live, record async.

**Format:** Screen + face cam (Loom or OBS)
**Length:** 10-20 min max (async = lower tolerance for long content)
**Structure:** Problem → Answer → Show (no long preamble)
**CTA:** "If you have questions, reply to this email / book a call / comment here"

**Difference from YouTube:**
- Less polished (internal Loom = okay with imperfections)
- More direct ("This is specifically relevant to your team because...")
- Context-specific (tailor to their situation, not a public general audience)

## Session Recording → Content Repurpose

If the live session was recorded:

| Recording → | Output |
|-------------|--------|
| Full recording (unedited) | Internal wiki / archive |
| Edited 30-min cut | YouTube (if permission granted) |
| Best 90-sec clip | LinkedIn post |
| Key Q&A moments | FAQ blog post ("Questions I get about X") |
| Slides + audio | Podcast episode (audio-only version) |
| Whiteboard / demo moments | Tutorial blog section |

**Permission check before publishing any recording:**
- [ ] All participants consented (if 121 or workshop)
- [ ] No sensitive data visible (client names, internal metrics)
- [ ] Recording quality is good enough for public (lighting, audio, no interruptions)

## Measurement: Did It Work?

For 121 / small group:
- Did they ask good questions? (engagement = understanding)
- Did they book a follow-up? (value perceived)
- Did they act on the next step? (practical impact)

For webinar:
- Attendance rate: registrants vs. attendees
- Drop-off: when did people leave? (if >40% leave early: pacing issue or wrong audience)
- Q&A quality: Were questions intelligent? (shows engagement vs. passive watching)
- Post-event: Did any convert? (follow-up meeting, purchase, referral)
