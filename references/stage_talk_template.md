## Opening (First 60 sec)

**Do NOT open with your bio.** Start with the room.

Pattern: [Problem/question for them] → [Why you're the right person to answer it] → [What they'll leave with]

> "How many of you have had an AI system give you a confident, completely wrong answer? [Pause for hands.] That's a context problem. And by the end of today, you'll know exactly why it happens and what to do about it."

**Speaker notes:**
- Make eye contact before speaking
- Pause after opening question — let room respond
- Don't rush this moment; let it land

## Section 4: Real World (3-5 min)

Evidence it works. Case study or personal story.

> "[Company / team / personal anecdote]. The problem was X. What they did: Y. Result: Z metric."

[SLIDE: Outcome visual — numbers, timeline, or quote]

**Why this story matters for THIS audience:**
- [For conference: positions you as practitioner, not theorist]
- [For all-hands: shows company has proof, not just vision]
- [For board: shows ROI is measurable, risk is manageable]

**Speaker notes:**
- Personal stories > company case studies (more credible, more memorable)
- If no personal story: use well-known company, attribute clearly
- Don't oversell — "This worked here; here's why it may work for you"

## Section 5: Close (2-3 min)

**Do NOT end with "questions?"** End with meaning.

Pattern: [What you said] → [Why it matters to THEM specifically] → [One thing to do]

> "We've covered [X]. If you only take one thing from this talk, it's: [soundbite]. This week, try [specific action]. Notice what changes."

[SLIDE: Single takeaway — one sentence, large type]

**Soundbite drop (enterprise talks):**
> "[Catchy, repeatable phrase]" — [Context for why it's powerful]

**Speaker notes:**
- Look up from notes at this moment; say it to the room
- Pause after soundbite (5-sec silence is not too long)
- Smile after closing line

## Q&A Preparation (10-15 min)

Pre-prepare answers to likely questions.

| Likely Question | Short Answer | Flag If Complex |
|-----------------|--------------|-----------------|
| "How much does this cost to implement?" | [1-2 sentences] | — |
| "Does this work at scale?" | [1-2 sentences + data] | — |
| "What tools do you use?" | [1-2 sentences] | — |
| "How long did this take your team?" | [Honest answer] | — |
| "[Objection or edge case]" | [Preemptive answer] | If needs longer: "Great question — let's talk after" |

**Q&A rules:**
- Repeat every question (audience may not have heard)
- Say "I don't know" when you don't know — adds credibility
- Hard question: "That's a longer answer — find me after"
- Off-topic: "Great question — slightly different track, happy to discuss offline"

## Slide Deck (Stage Version)

Different from regular deck: Designed for projection, not reading.

**Stage deck rules:**
- Max 6 words per slide (not 6 lines — 6 WORDS)
- Slide = visual anchor for speaker, not script for audience
- Font size: 60pt minimum
- Color: high contrast (white on dark or black on white — no grey-on-grey)
- No animations (remote clicker lag)

**Slide count:** 10-15 slides for 30-min talk

See `references/deck_template.md` for content; apply stage formatting rules above.

## Tailoring Notes Per Audience

**Conference (strangers, mixed):**
- Less assumption of shared context
- More time on problem framing (they may not know they have this problem)
- Business card/LinkedIn ask at the end

**All-Hands (internal team):**
- Can reference internal data ("We saw X in Q1")
- Less time on problem (they live it)
- More time on "here's what we're doing about it"
- End with action items for the team

**Board (execs, strategic):**
- Lead with business impact (not technology)
- Skip technical mechanism — stay in ROI, risk, market
- Three slides max per topic
- Have backup slides: anticipate "tell me more about X"

**121 (one exec, one-on-one):**
- See `references/live_session_template.md` (121 is its own format)
- Less formal; more conversation; less prepared remarks
- Prepare 5 key points; expect to cover 2-3 in real conversation

**Meetup / technical audience:**
- Can go deeper on mechanism
- Live demo expected (have it ready)
- Q&A will be detailed — prepare technical answers
- Community: end with "contribute / discuss / collaborate" not just "buy/hire"

## Speaker Performance Notes

**Pacing:**
- Slow down by 20% (adrenaline speeds up speech)
- Pause intentionally: after key line, before new section, after question
- Breathe before walking on stage

**Physical:**
- Hands: open and forward, not crossed or in pockets
- Movement: walk when transitioning sections; stand still when making key point
- Eye contact: hold for 3 sec per person (not a scan — a connection)

**Voice:**
- Project from diaphragm, not throat
- Vary pace: fast for narrative, slow for key insight
- Lower pitch = authority (calm, not rushed)

**When things go wrong:**
- AV fails: "Let me describe what you'd see on the slide — [describe it]"
- Forget your place: "Let me take a moment to recenter..." [pause + breathe]
- Hard question you don't know: "I don't know — that's a great test case; let me research and follow up"
- Running over time: skip Section 3, go straight to close: "I'm going to skip ahead to save time — here's the key takeaway..."
