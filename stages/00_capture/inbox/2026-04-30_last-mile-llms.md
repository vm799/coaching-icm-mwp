**Maps to ICM layers directly:**

### 3. "Managing Morphing Systems" = ICM's Remakeability

> "The morphing-systems mindset asks you to treat last quarter's pipeline as parts, not architecture. The structure can be rebuilt. The pieces inside it — the prompts, the skill files, the workflows — don't [change]. The structure morphs. The pieces inside it compound."

**Direct mapping to ICM:**
- Structure (pipeline) can be rebuilt with each new model/tool
- Pieces (CONTEXT.md files, voice guides, stage contracts) compound in value
- ICM's L0-L3 are the pieces. The pipeline (stages) is the structure.

**Enterprise angle:** "Your AI investment isn't in the workflow. It's in the skill files. The workflow will change. The skills compound."

### 4. "Last Mile" Framing — Humans as Quality + Judgment Layer

> "The model handles the bulk transit. You handle the last few feet. The last mile is the quality layer. It's the orchestration. It's the refinements that other humans relate to because a human shaped them. Models can produce volume. They can't yet produce the finish that makes another person trust the work. That gap is the job."

**V's teaching angle:** ICM's human review gates = the last mile built into the architecture. Stage contracts define exactly where humans add the last mile quality.

**V's enterprise angle:** "Your competitive advantage isn't the model. It's the last mile — the human judgment, domain expertise, and quality layer that makes AI output trustworthy. ICM builds that layer systematically."

**Systems thinking:** Humans move from production (high volume, low judgment) to last mile (low volume, high judgment). The feedback loop that matters is human → source correction, not human → output editing.
