## Critical Finding: YC #4 Is ICM/MWP

Tom Blomfield (YC partner, Monzo co-founder) wrote #4 verbatim:

> "Every company has critical know-how scattered everywhere. In people's heads, in old email threads, in Slack, in support tickets. The company works because humans vaguely remember where that knowledge is. AI agents cannot operate like that. [He wants] a system that pulls knowledge out of every fragmented source, structures it, keeps it current, and turns it into an executable skills file for AI. Not a search tool. Not a chatbot over documents. A living map of how a company actually works. Every company in the world will need one. The AI tools exist. The company brain layer does not yet."

## What This Signals About AI Perception

### The paradigm shift YC is betting on:

1. **From tool → system**: AI is no longer something you use. It's something you design.
2. **From copilot → autonomous**: 2023-25 was copilots. 2026+ is "skip the human entirely and just do the work."
3. **Bottleneck shifted**: Not model capability. Domain knowledge. Context architecture. (#4 states this explicitly.)
4. **Services > software**: Total services spend >> software spend. AI replaces services faster than software.
5. **Context layer = the new moat**: Whoever structures enterprise knowledge for agents wins. Not the model. The context.

### The "Company Brain" Shift
Most people think AI adoption = more powerful tools.
YC is saying: AI adoption = restructured knowledge. The companies that win will be those whose institutional knowledge is **structured for machines**, not just stored for humans.

That's V's entire practice. ICM is the methodology. MWP is the architecture.
